Are you ready to explore deep waters?

Octopuses inhabit various regions of the ocean, including coral reefs, pelagic waters, and the seabed; some live in the intertidal zone and others at abyssal depths. Most species grow quickly, mature early, and are short-lived.
Jellyfish are mainly free-swimming marine animals with umbrella-shaped bells and trailing tentacles, although a few are anchored to the seabed by stalks rather than being mobile. The bell can pulsate to provide propulsion for highly efficient locomotion.
Clownfish or anemonefish are fishes from the subfamily Amphiprioninae in the family Pomacentridae. Depending on the species, anemonefish are overall yellow, orange, or a reddish or blackish color, and many show white bars or patches.
C-Exploration is a Canadian-based organization that fights aquatic pollution in the Pacific Ocean, providing a safe environment for all underwater species of life!
